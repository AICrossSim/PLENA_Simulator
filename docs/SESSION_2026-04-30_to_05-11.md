## Problem Statement

The ATen compilation path (`compiler/aten/plena_frontend.py`) compiles HuggingFace models to PLENA ISA and validates them against a golden reference. It worked perfectly for clm-60m (hidden=384, 100% allclose at 1/5/10 layers) but SmolVLM2-256M (hidden=576) produced:
1. **All NaN** (before f32 fix)
2. **32% allclose** (after f32 fix)

---

## Root Causes Found

### 1. f16 Scalar Register Underflow → NaN (CRITICAL)

**What:** The emulator used IEEE f16 (1+5+10 bits, min subnormal ~6e-8) for scalar FP registers (`fp_reg`) and FPSRAM. SmolVLM2's attention scores have per-row spread ~530 after scaling. The online softmax accumulates `l = sum(exp(S-max))` — for rows where most scores are masked to -inf, `exp(-530) = 3.88e-39` which underflows f16 to zero. `1/l = 1/0 = Inf` → NaN propagates through the entire output.

**Why clm-60m wasn't affected:** Its attention scores are much smaller (per-row spread ~0.02 after scaling), so exp values stay within f16 range.

**Fix:** Change `fp_reg: [f16; 8]` → `[f32; 8]` and `fpsram: Vec<f16>` → `Vec<f32>`. PR #32.

**Why we can't avoid this:** The ISA defines what the hardware does. The emulator must faithfully execute it. f16 registers were wrong from the start — the RTL uses e6m5 (6-bit exponent, more dynamic range), the TOML declares e8m7. f32 is the pragmatic emulator choice.

### 2. Three-Way FP Precision Mismatch (DOCUMENTED)

| Storage | RTL | TOML | Emulator (before) | Emulator (after PR #32) |
|---------|-----|------|--------------------|--------------------------|
| Scalar FP regs | e6m5 (12-bit) | e8m7 (16-bit) | IEEE f16 (wrong) | f32 |
| FPSRAM | e6m5 | — | IEEE f16 (wrong) | f32 |
| Vector SRAM | e6m5 | e8m7 | bf16 via QuantTensor | bf16 (unchanged) |
| HBM | MXFP8 | MXFP8 | MXFP8 (correct) | MXFP8 (unchanged) |

George confirmed: sim validates assembly correctness; RTL sim does full precision validation.

### 3. Standard Comparison Drift (NOT A BUG)

**What:** The golden reference in `compile_hf_model` computes a float32 chain. The emulator stores bf16 in VRAM at every stage boundary. After 10+ chained operations, the accumulated precision difference exceeds `atol=0.2` for SmolVLM2's larger intermediate values.

**Why it's not fixable in the golden:**
- f32 matmul uses BLAS-optimized summation order
- Hardware M_MM sums in 64-element tile blocks
- Floating-point addition is NOT associative: `(a+b)+c ≠ a+(b+c)`
- Even computing the golden in bf16 throughout didn't help (28.92%) because `torch.matmul` bf16 uses different internal accumulation than the hardware's tile-based M_MM

**Solution:** VRAM-in-the-loop stage comparison validates the emulator by feeding its own intermediates back as golden input. Each segment matches at 100%.

### Days 3-4 (May 2-3): Chasing the 32% Gap

- **False lead #1:** MXFP8 scale not applied — disproven by unit test (scale works perfectly)

**Feedback comparison (the proof):**

## Key Learnings

1. **Unit tests > full simulation.** The 57K-line ISA takes 2-5 hours. Minimal ISA tests (10-7000 lines) take seconds to minutes and give definitive answers.

2. **Always verify your measurement before concluding there's a bug.** The "8x error" was comparing post-norm values against pre-norm golden. Wasted 2 days.

3. **VRAM-in-the-loop is the only viable comparison for large models.** Standard golden chain comparison inherently fails for models with large intermediate values due to matmul accumulation order differences.

4. **The emulator's f16 registers were fundamentally wrong.** Not matching any of the three specs (RTL e6m5, TOML e8m7, or any standard format). f32 is correct for the emulator's purpose of ISA validation.

5. **Don't kill background processes you're monitoring.** Multiple hours lost to accidentally SIGTERM-ing the emulator through wrapper scripts.
